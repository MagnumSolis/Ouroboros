# RBI Master Direction on Fraud Risk Management 2024
## Reference: RBI/DOS/2024-25/118 | DOS.CO.FMG.SEC.No.5/23.04.001/2024-25
## Date: July 15, 2024

## 1. Overview
The Reserve Bank of India has issued comprehensive "Revised Master Directions on Fraud Risk Management" that supersede all previous guidelines. This direction consolidates 36 existing circulars into a single framework.

## 2. Key Definitions
- **Fraud**: A dishonest act or omission, committed intentionally by any person, with the design to: (a) gain or give undue advantage; (b) defeat the interest of a person; (c) cause loss to a person.
- **Cyber Fraud**: A fraud committed using electronic means, including but not limited to, unauthorized access to computer systems or networks, phishing, vishing, smishing, or any other similar attack.

## 3. Reporting Timelines (Critical)
| Category | Amount Threshold | Reporting Deadline |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Individual Frauds | ₹1 lakh and above | Within 7 days of detection |
| All Frauds | ₹50 crore and above | Flash report within 24 hours |
| Staff Involved Frauds | Any amount | Within 3 days |

## 4. Fraud Prevention Framework
### 4.1 Three Lines of Defense Model
1. **First Line**: Business Units and Operations (Own and Control Risk)
2. **Second Line**: Risk Management and Compliance (Oversee and Challenge)
3. **Third Line**: Internal Audit (Independent Assurance)

### 4.2 Mandatory Controls
- Real-time transaction monitoring for high-value payments
- AI/ML-based anomaly detection systems
- Customer authentication using at least two factors for digital transactions above ₹2,000
- Cooling-off period of 24 hours for new beneficiary additions

## 5. Customer Protection Measures
- **Zero Liability**: Customer has zero liability if they report unauthorized transaction within 3 working days.
- **Limited Liability**: Liability capped at ₹10,000 if reported between 4-7 working days.
- **Full Liability**: Customer bears full loss if reported after 7 working days.

## 6. Penalties for Non-Compliance
- Monetary penalty up to ₹2 crore for first offense
- License suspension for repeated violations
- Personal liability of Chief Compliance Officer

## 7. Special Provisions for Digital Lending
- All digital lending apps must be registered with RBI
- Loan disbursement must be directly to borrower's bank account
- No third-party access to borrower's mobile contacts or gallery
- Maximum interest rate cap: MCLR + 4% for secured loans
